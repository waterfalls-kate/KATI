ABOUT SIBUYAN

  • Sibuyan Island is located in the Philippines, specifically in the MIMAROPA region, between the islands of Mindoro and Masbate. It is part of the province of Romblon and has an area of 465 square kilometers.


  • The island is known for its natural resources, including forests, mountains, and seas. It is also home to endemic species of plants and animals, such as orchids, birds, and butterflies.


  • The main economic activities on the island are agriculture, fishing, and mining. The island is rich in minerals such as nickel, chromite, and copper.


  • Sibuyan Island has a population of around 60,000 people, scattered across the municipalities of Magdiwang, San Fernando, Cajidiocan, and San Jose.


  • The island is also rich in culture and history. The indigenous people of Sibuyan have traditional practices and beliefs, such as rituals for planting and harvesting rice.


    • In recent years, Sibuyan Island has become popular among tourists due to its natural attractions, such as waterfalls, caves, and beaches. The island has the potential to become a major tourist destination in the Philippines.
